Got mushroom?

Our mission:

Whether you are firing up the grill for a weekend cookout or embarking on a quest to save a princess from an evil turtle-like creature, mushrooms can be the key ingredient for success! Our goal is to make identifying both delicious and potentially poisonous mushrooms as easy as possible. Utilizing the power of machine learning and the extensive dataset we've collected from UC Irvine's Machine Learning Repository, we've developed a sophisticated model capable of predicting whether a mushroom is edible or poisonous with precision.

Mushroom Facts:
  • A. phalloides is one of the most poisonous of all known mushrooms. It is estimated that as little as half a mushroom contains enough toxin to kill an adult human.
  • Matsutake mushrooms are the world's most expensive mushrooms. Their disappearing habitat in Japan causes the price to climb.
  • 'Death Cap' Mushrooms Pose Threat to dogs and humans and grow commonly in Wisconsin. If you see your pet eating a wild mushroom call your vet or poison control.
  • Scientists have classified 2,189 mushrooms as edible.
  • Some mushrooms glow in the dark. Bioluminescent mushrooms use light-emitting compounds to attract insects.
